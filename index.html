Arecibo Message Visualization

Mathematical Explanation

The Arecibo message consists of 1,679 binary digits (0s and 1s). The number 1,679 is a semiprime (a product of two prime numbers), 23 and 73. This allows the message to be arranged into a 23 by 73 grid.

The message includes:

  • Numbers 1 to 10
  • Atomic numbers of elements hydrogen, carbon, nitrogen, oxygen, and phosphorus
  • Formulas for the chemical compounds that make up the nucleotides of DNA
  • Estimated number of DNA nucleotides and a graphic of the double helix structure of DNA
  • Dimension of an average man, a graphic figure of a human being, and the human population of Earth
  • Graphic of the Solar System, indicating which planet the message is coming from
  • Graphic of the Arecibo radio telescope and the dimension of the transmitting antenna dish
